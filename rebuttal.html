Massively Parallel Training in Simulation


We implement custom simulation of closed loop chains that enables us to simulate the Digit robot in Isaac Gym which was not possible before (please see Section IV B of the paper for details). We further implement distributed training that allows us to parallelize training across multiple GPUs and thousands of enviroments. These combined, enable us to collect 10 Billion samples in a day on a single machine. To facilitate future research, we will make all of our system code publicly available.


​

Transformers for Humanoid Locomotion

We train a Transformer-based controller that predicts future actions autoregressively from the history of past observations and actions. We hypothesize that the history of observations and actions implicitly encodes the information about the world that a powerful Transformer model can use to adapt its behavior dynamically at test time. For example, the model can use the history of desired vs actual states to figure out how to adjust its actions to better achieve future states.

Attention Map Visualization

Our Transformer has a context length of 16 and 4 self-attention heads. To better understand our controller, we visualize how the attention changes over time while the robot is walking. The last index is the most recent. We observe that our model pays attention from close to far history information across time and is well aligned with the walking phase cycle.
